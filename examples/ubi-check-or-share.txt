The world's biggest UBI fan helped run one of the biggest UBI studies ever.

Then the data came back.

People got $1,000/month for 3 years. No strings.

The result was not the sci-fi utopia version:

- Recipients worked a little less
- Stayed unemployed longer
- Earned less outside the transfer
- Used much of the extra time for leisure

That does not mean cash is useless. It means cash is not the whole answer.

The more interesting turn: Sam Altman moved from "send people checks" toward "everyone should participate in the upside."

In other words: not just UBI.

Ownership.

Alaska's oil dividend is the clue. Same money, totally different psychology.

If AI really does end most work, the design choice matters:

Do people become dependents?

Or shareholders?

CHECK or SHARE?
